Journey and Milestones

OUR JOURNEY

● Smaran, as a Company has been putting consistent efforts in research and development of Innovative and Sustainable Green Technology. For years now team SMARAN has been working on economical and scalable technologies to convert the abundantly available biomass into Bio-fuel and other bio-products.

● Smaran aims at optimal utilization of surplus biomass which is currently being burnt in an unscientific way leading to enormous pollution and loss of valuable carbon economy. The pioneering vision of team SMARAN is to Convert Waste to Wealth. The vision is to make India self-reliant on Industrial and Transportation Fuels using Renewable Agricultural wastes and residues.

  • 2020

    Team SMARAN embarked on a Trans Indian Journey to understand the status, logistics, and economy of production for 1 st generation Bio Diesel from Non-Edible Oils and concluded making Biodiesel from non-edible oil seeds is not a viable solution

  • 2021

    Team SMARAN along with consensus and consultation of specialists from the Petroleum Refining Industry, Professors in Fluid Dynamics, Chemistry Doctorates in Nano Particles, experts in heterogeneous catalysis, Professors in Mechanical Engineering, and Professional MBAs brainstormed and concluded that 4th Generation Bio Fuel Technology which uses abundant Bio Mass available and converts it into Bio Fuel is the way forward


    Team SMARAN decided to incubate at Siddaganga TBI, SIT Campus, and under the guidance of the Department of Chemical Engineering SIT, Tumkur carried out the required Laboratory trials to establish the Proof of Concept (POC). A 5 Liter/per day Pilot Plant was put up to validate the results

  • 2022

    Upon successful completion of Pilot trials SMARAN put up an Industrial Level Demonstration Unit at Bio Fuel Plant of SIT with a capacity of 1000 Liters per day and successfully demonstrated its Proprietary Engineering and Technological Skills

  • 2023-2024

    ● Establishment of Quality Excellence for Industrial Scale production.
    ● Work in progress to establish quality norms as per BIRDC Karnataka norms and as well as BIS VI norms.
    ● Engine test for Industrially produced fuel at BIRDC SIT Tumkur and Vehicle Run Tests planned for October 2023.
    ● R&D Collaboration with Department of Chemistry NIE Mysore and SJCE Mysore for Advanced Catalysis and Polymer Chemistry.
