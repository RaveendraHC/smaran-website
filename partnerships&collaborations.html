Partnerships and Collaborations

Our Partners

Siddaganga Institute of Technology, Tumakuru


Siddaganga Institute of Technology is a premier Institute established in 1963 at Tumkur .It has been accredited by NAAC with A++ Grade and has been awarded a Diamond college rating from QS I∙GAUGE. It continuously works towards imparting Quality Education by establishing effective learning processes to produce competent engineers and managers with high professional ethics and societal responsibility.

Smaran has Incubated in the Technology Incubation centre of SIT since 2021. Smaran has carried Laboratory Trails in co-ordination with Department of Chemical Engineering. It has been doing continuous research and development work in the stream of quality assurance in partnership with the Bio-Fuel research plant of SIT.

National Institution of Engineering, Mysuru


The National Institute of Engineering (NIE) has been at the forefront of technical education in Karnataka, through the continuous implementation of Outcome Based Education (OBE), ensuring that its graduates are able to face up to the challenges demanded by the engineering profession.

The National Institute of Engineering (NIE), established in the year 1946, today stands among top engineering colleges in the country that include IITs and NITs as per National Institution Ranking Framework (NIRF-2023).
Smaran has collaborated with NIE for Development of Nano catalyst for Smarans’ range of products like Bio-fuels, Bio- Phenyls and Derivates. It has been closely working with the Bio-fuel plant of NIE for Engine Testing and other International Bio-diesel Quality checks.

Sri Jayachamarajendra College of Engineering, Mysuru


SMARAN has collaborated with JCE which is one of the premium colleges in Mysore. It has a legacy of training talent from all over India in Engineering for the last 6 decades.

SJCE has successfully completed World Bank assisted TEQIP – I scheme of Government of India as a lead institute in which the emphasis has been on infrastructure and is part of TEQIP II scheme in which priority is on post graduate education, research and innovation.
Smaran has collaborated with Chemistry Department of SJCE . Continuous research and development is being done in field of Bio-mulch. Bio-mulch can be used during the crop cycle wherein by the end of the crop cycle the mulch gets bio-degraded leaving the farmer ready for the next cycle. Presently non-bio degradable polymer mulches are used and burnt causing huge environmental issues.

Strategic Relationships

Smaran is helping present day 1st generation bio fuel producers, who are unable to source raw materials or provided with pyrolysis based crude pyrolysis oil and catalytic refining to convert into ASTM grade bio diesel.
The capacity is ranging from 3 tons/day to 25 tons/day of bio diesel. Hence unviable 1st generation producers are now able to and eligible to supply tenders to Petroleum Refineries like IOC, HPCL, BPCL, MRPL, local KSRTC depots and Railways.


SMARAN is in continuous engagement for supply of bio-diesel with some of the big transporters and software companies with Pan India Presence, who are interested in carbon credits and the taking of Biofuels
It is also having strategic allowances for supplying waste agri bio-mass with large suppliers and AK Agri solutions as strategic channel partners
